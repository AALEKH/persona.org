Stop worrying about storing passwords.

Mozilla Persona verifies users’ email addresses and lets them sign in to your site in as few as two clicks, so you can say goodbye to the headaches that come with password storage.

Forget passwords

Securing your users’ passwords takes resources away from your primary focus: maintaining and developing your application. But no matter how well you do it, you can still get hurt when people use the same passwords on other, less secure, sites. Although this is a risk you can’t control, your reputation still suffers when password compromise happens.

Breezy to implement

Adding Persona to your site can take as little as 15 minutes. Include our Javascript shim on your website, make two JS calls and one CURL on the server to verify, and you’re in business. Detailed instructions are, of course, maintained on the Mozilla Developer Network.

Low commitment

Persona’s not a silo from which there is no escape. Users always provide a verified email address which makes it easy to transition to or away from Persona.

Supported browsers / devices

Today a service, tomorrow a standard

Currently Mozilla supports Persona by providing the supporting infrastructure, but soon it will be available natively in products like Firefox and other browser add-ons. We are working towards making it a standard that can operate independently of Mozilla services, leveraging existing standards like HTTP, JSON and JWT. Until that time, Persona is just a Javascript call away.
