Stop worrying about ●●●●ing passwords.

Mozilla Persona lets users sign into your site simply by verifying their email address, relieving you of the headaches that come with storing passwords.

They hate them. You should too.

Compromised passwords burn everybody. Unfortunately, people are using the same passwords on your site as they are on other sites; this means their data is only as safe with you as it is on the least secure site they use. To further aggravate the situation, safe password storage is an ever-evolving practice that draws resources away from your primary focus: maintaining and developing your own application.

Today a service, tomorrow a standard.

Currently Mozilla supports Persona by providing the supporting infrastructure, but soon it will be available natively in products like Firefox and other browser add-ons. We are working towards making it a standard that can operate independently of Mozilla services, leveraging existing standards like HTTP, JSON and JWT. Until that time, Persona is just a Javascript call away.

Password-free for 700 million

Persona allows users to sign into sites with any email address, but is especially convenient for Gmail & Yahoo addresses where no new password need be created.

What else?

Breezy to implement

Include our Javascript shim on your website, make two JS calls and one CURL on the server to verify, and you’re in business. Detailed instructions are, of course, maintained on the Mozilla Developer Network.

Improved privacy

Persona is developed by Mozilla, a not-for-profit company trusted throughout the Web community. Our goal is to create technologies that balance an open Web platform with people's privacy.

Mozilla to the rescue

Mozilla’s mission is to promote openness, innovation & opportunity on the Web. And just like our flagship product Firefox, Persona champions you – your security, privacy, and the quality of your online life.
